We intend to help you navigate the confusing welfare and social support services provided by Centrelink, and state and territory governments during the Coronavirus crisis.

Website will be launching in the coming days.


Update:


The Australian Government indicated on Sunday 29 March that they are looking at expanding income support through a mechanism similar to a wage subsidy. This will be for people who have had their income or work impacted by the Covid-19 Crisis.

It is unclear at this stage if this will include people who have already been impacted and who have already applied for Centrelink.

Traditionally, Australians would get a flat rate payment through Centrelink, regardless of how much you earned prior to losing work.

A wage subsidy is a different approach to welfare than we are used to in Australia. It provides income support to you, based on your income prior to being out of work. Countries such as Canada, Denmark, the United Kingdom, France and New Zealand have announced government measures to provide a wage subsidy during the Covid-19 Crisis.

A wage subsidy will require your employer to continue to pay you at 70% - 80% of your current salary, which will be reimbursed (paid for) by the Australian Government. A condition will be that your employer re-hires you at the end of the crisis.


Interim advice


Already on Centrelink payment

Wait for our update following the Government’s announcement later this week about what you should do.

About to apply, or waiting for an outcome from Centrelink

Continue as is. At this stage the details including the start date of the new wage subsidy package are unknown. Applying for, or waiting for an outcome from Centrelink will likely not impact a future wage subsidy program. You will most likely be better to get into the current system now, and make a change into the new system later, if needed.

Have not applied for a Centrelink payment

  • You have had income or work impacted by Covid-19: If you have not looked into starting an application for a Centrelink payment it may be best to wait until the announcement later this week to assess your options.

  • You have not yet had income or work impacted by Covid-19, but may be impacted in the future: Rest assured that your options will be clearer in the coming days, depending on your situation. Be sure to visit this site again following the update.

Hold tight, while we wait for the government to announce their latest package. We expect this announcement on Tuesday 31 March or Wednesday 01 April.
